# BarBrawl — The Game

A readable overview you can take out with you.

*Last updated: end of the big build push. `main` is at a state where
the full game-logic layer is complete and tested.*

---

## The Pitch

**Every real bar near you is a mini-dungeon.** Walk up, fight through
procedurally generated rooms, claim it, station a defender, earn while
you sleep. Diablo's build depth meets Pokémon GO's location-first
world, set at your local watering holes.

You don't need to drink to play. The Operator class is a first-class
citizen, designed for designated drivers, sober players, recovery,
pregnancy — anyone who's *at* a bar but not drinking. Spec §1 bakes
this in as a non-negotiable principle.

---

## The Core Loop

1. **Open the map.** Bars near you appear as colored markers by type.
2. **Walk into range** (100m). Tap the bar to see its boss, defenders,
   and CHALLENGE button.
3. **Fight through 3-5 rooms.** Layouts regenerate daily, same for
   every player attacking that bar on that day.
4. **Beat the boss.** Loot drops. XP awards. You now own the bar.
5. **Station a defender.** Pick a character from your roster — they
   stay, earn coins hourly, and fight back when another player shows
   up.
6. **Come back tomorrow.** Daily reset, new room layouts, fresh loot.

## Your Roster (7 characters, one per class)

Every account owns all seven from day one. They level independently,
have separate inventories and skill allocations, share your gold
wallet. You pick which character to bring to each fight based on the
bar type, your loadout, and the vibe.

| Class | Archetype | Stats (HP/ATK/DEF/SPD/LUCK) | Specialty Bar |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operator** 🎯 | Precision / crit specialist | 110 / 11 / 11 / 11 / 11 | Universal — no specialty |
| **Bouncer** 🛡️ | Heavy tank-striker | 130 / 12 / 14 / 7 / 6 | Dive bars |
| **Hexwright** 🔮 | Curse / debuff caster | 85 / 8 / 6 / 12 / 15 | Wine bars |
| **Duelist** ⚔️ | Burst hybrid / rhythm mixups | 95 / 11 / 8 / 13 / 13 | Cocktail lounges |
| **Medic** ⚕️ | Sustain healer / DoT | 115 / 9 / 10 / 10 / 11 | Breweries |
| **Ghost** 🌫️ | Evasion / chaos / speed | 100 / 10 / 8 / 14 / 13 | Nightclubs |
| **Gambler** 🎲 | High-variance / wager | 100 / 10 / 8 / 11 / 17 | Pubs |

Each class has a **unique resource meter and action economy** — these
are what make classes feel genuinely different to play, not just their
stat spreads.

### Operator — Focus
Precision-sniper archetype. Resource **Focus** builds passively (+1/turn,
+2/crit) and is spent on guaranteed-hit skills. *Clean Strike* always
hits, *Bullseye* lets crits ignore half defense, keystone **Absolute
Clarity** makes every hit a crit in exchange for -40% base ATK.

### Bouncer — Grit
Absorb-to-bank specialist. Resource **Grit** is generated by taking
damage (+1 per 10 HP absorbed). Unique action: spend a turn *absorbing*
→ an enemy gets a free swing at you, but your next turn has **two
actions** banked. Keystone **Last Stand** revives you once at 30% HP
the first time you'd die.

### Hexwright — Curse Stacks
Debuff amplifier. **Curse Stacks** live on enemies, not you — and some
skills cost *their* stacks rather than yours. Harbinger adds +10% crit
per stack; *Blood Sigil* lands a 180% damage hit that applies every
curse at once. Keystone **Coven** makes curses spread to anyone
defending the bar you're attacking.

### Duelist — Tempo
Perfect-rhythm rewarder. **Tempo** gains +1 on every perfect-timing hit
and decays 1/turn if unused. Chain **3 consecutive perfects** and you
get a free bonus action next turn. Keystone **Third Strike** auto-crits
every 3rd attack but halves the damage of your non-crits.

### Medic — Reserve
Overheal banker. When a heal would take you over max HP, the excess
converts to **Reserve** you can spend later. *Full Workup* heals +
cleanses + buffs in one skill. Keystone **Code Blue** auto-revives you
at full HP once per day in exchange for -50% gold earned.

### Ghost — Momentum
Dodge-chain blitzer. **Momentum** builds on every dodge. Unique action:
trade half your current SPD for an **immediate bonus action**. Keystone
**Juke** gives you 50% dodge chance, but your HP cap drops to 50%.

### Gambler — Chips (NEW CLASS)
All-in variance specialist. **Chips** tick up per action and especially
on crits. Every skill can be declared "all-in" — a coin flip for 2×
damage on win, 0× on loss. *Roll of the Dice* just deals 50-200%
random on every tap. Keystone **House Always Wins** doubles your LUCK
but reduces every other stat by 20%.

## Combat

Turn-based with **optional rhythm input**. Each turn:

1. Pick one of your 3 equipped skills (or a basic attack).
2. A marker slides across a 1.2-second bar.
3. Tap to lock in your timing.

| Tap landing | Quality | Damage multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| 47-53% of bar (gold zone) | **PERFECT** | 2.0× |
| 40-60% (green zone) | Good | 1.5× |
| Outside green zone | Miss | 0.5× |
| Didn't tap | Auto / AFK-safe | 1.0× |

Damage formula (server-authoritative, never trust the client):

```
base      = (ATK + level × 2) × skill multiplier
afterBeat = base × rhythm multiplier + variance
afterDef  = max(1, afterBeat − enemy DEF)
if crit:    afterDef × 1.8    (Ghost passive: × 2.5)
```

Crit chance = `LUCK / 80 + crit buffs`. A Gambler with 17 LUCK starts
at ~21% crit; a Hexwright ends up there too thanks to their 15 LUCK.

Defenders use the **same combat math on auto** — they tap every
rhythm as `Ok` (1.0×), so active play is always meaningfully stronger
than AFK but not game-breakingly so.

## Skill Trees (21 trees, 189 nodes)

Each class has 3 trees × 9 nodes = 27 nodes. The shape is uniform so
the UI feels consistent:

```
Tier 1:  [small] [small]          (stat starters, no prereqs)
Tier 2:  [active] [small]         (requires Tier 1)
Tier 3:      [notable]            (build-defining passive)
Tier 4:  [active] [small]         (requires Notable)
Tier 5:      [notable]
Tier 6:      [keystone]           (game-changing trade-off)
```

You earn 1 skill point per character level. Level cap is **1000**,
designed to take a casual player ~10 years — endgame is *not* maxing,
it's building out your roster of 7 characters with diverse builds.

Node types:
- **Small** (+3 ATK, +4% crit, etc.) — the prereq glue.
- **Notable** ("+20% ATK when HP > 80%") — cluster payoffs.
- **Active** (combat buttons) — you equip up to 3 at a time.
- **Keystone** — one per tree, always carries a penalty to balance
  the power. ABSOLUTE CLARITY: every hit crits, -40% ATK. HOUSE
  ALWAYS WINS: LUCK doubled, everything else -20%. IMMOVABLE: take
  50% less damage, can never crit.

## Loot & Gear

**Path of Exile + Borderlands + Destiny 2 hybrid**, account-bound.

Five slots: **Weapon, Outfit, Footwear, Trinket, Mark** (Mark is where
Metroidvania resistance items live — see below).

Every item has five layers:

1. **Base** defines the implicit — what the item *is*. Brass Knuckles
   give +15% unarmed damage; Cue Stick gives +12% damage at long reach;
   Switchblade gives +15% crit. Farming a specific base matters.
2. **Rarity** — Common (no affixes), Uncommon (1), Rare (2), Epic (3),
   Legendary (3 + guaranteed class anointment). Drop rates: 60/27/9/3/1.
3. **Tiered affixes** (20 concepts × 4 tiers = 80 total). T1 (best
   rolls) only appears at item level 80+. Bar tier 6 bars drop the best.
4. **Class anointment** (Legendary-only) — the thing that makes a
   Legendary a **build item** instead of a stat bump. It references
   a specific skill-tree node. "Last Call" brass knuckles: Bouncer's
   Grit generation +50% while HP < 30%. "Open Bout" switchblade:
   Duelist's Tempo cap raised from 5 to 8.
5. **Corruption** (endgame) — optional Vaal-Orb-style risk on any item.
   Adds an implicit mod, locks the item from further crafting.

**Focusing currency** lets you re-roll a specific affix slot. Earned
from play. Destiny 2's "god-roll focusing" pattern, adapted.

**No blockchain for launch.** Account-bound by default. A future
in-game "Back Bar Market" will allow limited soft-trading for Common/
Uncommon gear — no Legendaries, no crypto, all in-game gold.

## Metroid-Style Gating

Tier 3 and below bars are always enterable. **Tier 4+** bars deal a
specific damage type based on theme:

| Bar theme | Damage type |
|---|---|
| Dive | Blunt |
| Pub | Edged |
| Sports | Impact |
| Cocktail | Toxic |
| Wine | Shadow |
| Brewery | Heat |
| Nightclub | Sonic |

You need a **Resistance Mark** for the matching type to survive.
Marks drop from Tier 3 clears — so a Tier-4 Dive Bar requires a
*Hardwood Ward* from any Tier-3 dive you've already cleared.

Some bars have a sealed **VIP Room** behind the boss — an extra floor
with better loot tables. Keys drop from a **different** bar of the
same theme, creating cross-bar progression puzzles: to fully clear The
Rusty Nail you might need a key from Mission Blackout.

Grayed-out bars on the map = locked. You can see them (the Metroid
"glimpse rule"), but can't enter yet.

## Progression

- **XP curve**: `floor(80 × level^1.45)`. Level 1 → 2 = 80 XP. Level
  10 → 11 = 2,254. Level 100 → 101 = 63,546. Level 1000 is the cap.
  *(Spec narrative lists larger numbers at high level — the formula
  is authoritative.)*
- **Per level**: +6 HP, +2 ATK, +1 DEF, +0.5 SPD, 1 skill point.
- **Mastery tracks** — 7 bar types × 7 characters = **49 tracks**.
  Tier rewards at 1/5/15/30 clears. 30-clear Master tier = +51 HP,
  +3 ATK, +6 DEF, title.
- **Daily refresh** — first clear of a bar each day = 100% rewards,
  2nd = 50%, 3rd+ = 25%.
- **First-ever clear bonus**: 2× XP.
- **Underdog bonus**: 2× XP for bars 10+ levels above yours.
- **Respec**: costs level² gold (L10 = 100g, L100 = 10k g). Every 10
  levels grants 5 free respec tokens.

## Defenders

After claiming a bar, station one of your 7 characters.

- **Max 7 stationed** across your whole account. Choose wisely.
- **2 coins / hour per defender**, capped at **75 coins / day**
  account-wide.
- **15% of every attacker's XP** comes to you passively when someone
  challenges your bar — win or lose.
- **HP decays 5% per day.** Feed them consumables remotely to refill.
- At 0 HP, they walk home carrying their accrued coins and XP.
- **1-hour cooldown** between recall and re-station.
- Stats **snapshot at placement** — a later respec or level-up
  doesn't retroactively buff a stationed defender.

## Consumables

Packed up to 4 per bar run. Unused return to stash after the fight.

- **Small Brew** — Heal 30% HP (common)
- **House Special** — Heal 70% HP (uncommon)
- **Shot of Courage** — +40% ATK 3 turns (uncommon)
- **Iron Tonic** — +50% DEF 3 turns (uncommon)
- **Focus Vial** — +30% crit 3 turns (rare)
- **Emergency Elixir** — Auto-revive at 50% HP, once per battle (rare)
- **Palette Cleanser** — Remove all debuffs (uncommon)

**Crafting**: combine lower-rarity items into consumables. 8 recipes
cover every consumable + an alt path for Emergency Elixir.

**Never purchasable with real money.** Sources: daily login, quests,
battle drops, crafting.

## Multiplayer (v1.5, deferred from launch)

**Open Raid System** — a Helldivers-style SOS beacon adapted for geo.

Anyone at a bar can *ignite a raid*: promotes the normal run to a
harder co-op variant with a boss at 3× HP and better loot. Igniting
pushes a notification to every logged-in player within **200m**. 90-
second join window, 2-6 players.

Design principles baked in:
- **No leader**, no kick-vote (kick-grief is the biggest failure mode
  among strangers).
- **Personal loot** — every player independently rolls against the
  raid table. No kill-stealing, no loot drama.
- **Contribution-scaled roll quality** (not eligibility) — carries
  get better odds, everyone gets something.
- **Silent-join toggle** — join without voice/chat. Critical for
  stranger lobbies.
- **Daily raid passes** (3 free/day, earned not purchased) — copying
  Pokémon GO's window cadence without its paid-pass feel-bad.

## Global Events

- **Weekly World Bosses** — Saturday 8pm local, a random high-tier
  bar in each region becomes a **Titan** for 48 hours. HP in the
  millions — the community chips it down. Damage contribution sorts
  you into tier: top 1% (epic drop), top 10% (rare), top 50%
  (uncommon), participant (common). 7 regions = 7 Titans/week.
- **Seasonal Challenges** — rotating themed windows:
  *Pub Crawl Week* (2× XP at pubs), *Brewery Fest* (exclusive
  cosmetics at breweries), *Operator's Month* (2× XP for Operators),
  *Wine Weekend, Neon Nights, Bouncer Week, Gambler's Night*.
  Effects stack **multiplicatively** — a Pub Crawl Week + Operator's
  Month Operator at a pub = 4× XP.
- **Monthly Crawl Pass** — Fortnite-style. 50 tiers, 10K XP each
  (500K to complete). All rewards cosmetic. ~$9.50 to unlock, returns
  more coins than it costs if completed.
- **Daily Quests** — 3 rotated daily from a 14-quest pool. Rewards
  25-150 XP. Pool includes: boss hunts, no-consumable wins, perfect-
  hit counts, fast clears, underdog wins, bar-type win-streaks.
- **Login Streaks** — 50+ gold daily base. Milestones at days 3
  (Small Brew), 7 (House Specials + respec token), 14 (Focus Vial),
  30 (Emergency Elixir + 3 tokens + "Regular" title).
- **City Tournaments** — monthly leaderboards by defender coins
  earned. Top 100 in each city get unique frames + titles.

## Monetization

### What we sell
- Character skins
- Emotes / victory dances / taunts
- Name frames / avatars
- Monthly Crawl Pass (~$9.50, all cosmetic)
- Bar Dashboard Pro for venue owners ($29/month B2B — analytics,
  custom boss names, happy hour events, featured placement)

### What we NEVER sell
XP boosts, stats, gear, consumables, respec tokens, level skips,
extra defender slots, premium skills, energy refills, or *anything*
that provides a gameplay advantage. Ever.

### Target numbers
- **Year 1**: 100K MAU × 30% conversion × $25 avg/year ≈ **$750K**
- **Year 3**: 5M MAU × 40% conversion × $40 avg/year ≈ **$80M**
- Fortnite benchmark: 77% conversion at $84/player/year

## Ethics (non-negotiable — spec §1)

1. **Zero pay-to-win, ever.** Cosmetic-only revenue.
2. **Drinking is optional.** The Operator is equally viable. We
   never reward real-world alcohol purchases. No receipt scanning.
3. **Any character can attempt any bar.** No gatekept content.
4. **Power comes from depth, not underleveled enemies.** Level 500
   crushes a Level 500 bar because of 500 levels of accumulated
   advantages, not because the bar got weaker.
5. **Daily midnight reset** keeps the world alive.
6. **Server-authoritative, always.** Assume players will try to
   cheat — GPS spoof, modified client, replay attacks — and design
   every system so they can't succeed.

## What's NOT Built (and Why)

Everything remaining is **UI** (rendering, input, animation) or
**infra** (deployment, external accounts). None of it is architectural
— it plugs in cleanly once the blockers lift.

**Blocked on distribution target:**
- Rhythm UI (the tap-bar animation)
- Skill tree SVG viewer
- Map screen (pick Mapbox native vs Leaflet web)
- Battle screen (React layer on top of the combat engine)
- Roster / inventory / stash UI

**Blocked on Supabase project (capital):**
- Edge function deployment
- Real auth / character persistence
- Battle state persistence

**Blocked on Apple/Google dev accounts (capital):**
- Push notifications
- Store builds (if native)
